# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Opportune a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that support an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- showing empathy and kindness;
- respecting different viewpoints and experiences;
- giving and accepting constructive feedback;
- taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes;
- focusing on what is best for users and the project;
- protecting other people's privacy and personal data.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language, imagery, or attention;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- pressuring users to share resumes, credentials, job-search records, or personal circumstances;
- deliberate disruption of Issues, reviews, or project infrastructure;
- conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This code applies in all Opportune project spaces and when a person officially represents the project in public.

## Reporting

For ordinary moderation concerns, open a minimal Issue asking for private maintainer contact. Do not place sensitive details or personal data in the public Issue.

Security vulnerabilities must use GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting as described in `SECURITY.md`.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and fairly. Maintainers will respect the reporter's privacy and disclose details only as needed to investigate and respond.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take actions including:

1. **Correction** — a private or public request to change inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — a time-limited restriction from interaction or contribution.
4. **Permanent restriction** — permanent removal from project participation.

The response will consider impact, context, intent, prior behavior, and whether harm was repaired.
